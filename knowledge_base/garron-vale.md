# Garron Vale

Type: character

## Overview

Garron Vale watches over Caelan Veyr while concealing his mysterious past tying him into the stories of Arcton Veyr and Caelan Veyr's father, Darius Veyr. After Garron Vale takes Caelan Veyr to Elyndra, the latter learns that Garron Vale is revered as one of the greatest oathsworn who ever lived due to serving Arcton Veyr on his successful pilgrimage. He becomes an oathsworn for Elyra Noctis, Arcton Veyr's daughter, on her pilgrimage and is, in many ways, the party's mentor, as the others come to him for advice or ask for his permission.

## Details

Garron Vale wears a brown shoulder pad on his left shoulder intricately decorated with tan, green, and blue patterns with a beaded ornament dangling from it. He wears black pants and shoes that have brown straps and triangular metal plating. Garron Vale wears a black shirt with a high gray collar with intersecting brown straps. His hair is black with gray streaks held back by a gold ribbon and he has some stubble on his chin.

Garron Vale is a strict, no-nonsense warrior with a dry wit who carries himself with grace and dignity, maintaining many subtle mannerisms of a traditional samurai. He is the strong silent type, speaking when he feels it's necessary—usually offering his knowledge and insight in relevant situations or sharing stories of his past experiences as a young oathsworn. As a result of his devotion to High Invoker Arcton Veyr, Garron Vale is idolized across Elyndra by many aspiring oathsworn.

Garron Vale proves his imperishable loyalty to Arcton Veyr and Darius Veyr, but beneath the silence, he is outraged over the events of ten years ago. He has become cynical, having little-to-no respect for The Luminous Order. Despite his usually collected demeanor, Garron Vale harbors feelings of self-loathing. He claims to Caelan Veyr and Torren Kaid that though he is seen as a great oathsworn, he considers his past self (like them) foolish and young.

Garron Vale exhibits some fatherly tendencies with Caelan Veyr and Elyra Noctis due to the promise he made to Darius Veyr and Arcton Veyr that he would look after their children. Garron Vale is patient with Caelan Veyr's childish behavior and insists that he be accepted as an oathsworn to Elyra Noctis, much to others' surprise. Caelan Veyr reminds him of Darius Veyr and Garron Vale enjoys pointing out the similarities, much to Caelan Veyr's annoyance. Garron Vale becomes irritated at hold-ups during the pilgrimage, although he ultimately lets the party do as they please, provided that it doesn't infringe on the journey. He does not hesitate to enter shops operated by the Relicborn or use relic engines, despite The Luminous Order's opposition of them.
